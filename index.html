Cultural Diversity

For the purpose of this visualisation, ethnicities are: The non-Australian and non-English population

Cultural diversity in Australia overall is still surprisingly low, with the majority of people in Australia claiming either English or Australian heritage. About 25% of Australia's population consists of people who identify within a group that is not either Australian or English in heritage.

The greatest concentration of ethnic peoples are in the cities of Sydney and Melbourne, with most of the population in other cities being much lower.

Religious Diversity

Australians for the most part appear to be either Christians or Atheists, with Christians being seperated into different denominations for the sake of the census. There exists many other groups in Australia, however they are extremely low in number compared to Christianity, with some religions having less members than some denominations of Christianity.

Within Christianity, the largest denomination is Catholicism, which arguably is an entirely seperate religion to Christianity despite sharing similar beliefs. The largest denomination within Christianity are the Anglicans, with all other denominations being relatively small in number compared to that both these groups.
